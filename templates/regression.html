Moving average is the average price of a stock over a set period of time. It’s a lagging indicator which means that we use past prices to predict the future price. It’s effectively showing the trend of a stock, since it smooths out short-term price fluctuations, it is unable to show the full extent of the changes in stock price, which means it fails to predict stock price.

We extracted all the related information from field DATE, which includes: ‘Year’, ‘Month’, ‘Week’, ‘Day’, ‘Dayofweek’, ‘Dayofyear’, ‘Is_month_end’, ‘Is_month_start’, ‘Is_quarter_end’, ‘Is_quarter_start’, ‘Is_year_end’ and ‘Is_year_start’. Those features are input to your model and the output is adj_close price. The linear regression also indicates the trend of a stock, however, it fails to predict the price.

DISCLAIMER: The current classification reports shown above for three models are for Apple stock only, different stocks yields different accuracy reports using the model. Due to the constraint of handling gigantic dataset in Jupyter Notebook, we can only run the model on one specific stock at a time. In the future, our team will deploy Spark to better handle big datasets.
